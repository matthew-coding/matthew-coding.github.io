Learning Reflection

If I had to be realistic, being a front end web developer seems like a pretty cool job, it’s a field im exceptionally new to as you can see from the quality of my blog, but it's been pretty fulfilling so far just being able to make something like this. If I'm allowed to dream I would absolutely love to get into something like game development, it's one of those passions I've had for such a long time that I always have the thought in the back of my mind of just dropping everything to go learn about it.

I would love to learn effective time management, it's one of those skills I've lacked a lot throughout my life, and the somewhat self-directed study of the foundations course is really pushing me in that area. Good communication is another one, I have been described as an introverted grumpy cat by my flat mate and she probably isn't wrong.

I think I'm very good at working on things that I'm passionate about, which is a strength and a weakness, and I'm pretty good at co-operating with others, although in a group work setting I do tend to follow others rather than submitting my own ideas. Time management as I've talked about is another area I'm weak in and I do get easily distracted.

I've thought about it for a while and while I'm studying at home at least I'm just trying to limit possible distractions, when I'm working I'll turn off my social media, close any apps that could present a distraction via pop up notifications or anything like that.

I've already given up doing several things that I knew wouldn’t be compatible while I'm studying at Dev Academy, I've changed or temporarily stopped several social obligations, and have ceased working during the study period, and I will commit to communication with my cohorts and facilitators, and always treating them with the respect they deserve.

I expect the facilitation team will be there to help talk me through any problems I might be facing with the course, and maybe a little bit of encouragement, so far they’ve been doing a pretty stellar job.

I don’t have really many block out times that I can think of, the only two things outside of Dev Academy I seriously need to commit time to are exercising, which I can be flexible with (thank you 24 hour gyms), and any social obligations I have to my friends, which take place outside of course hours anyway, and Ive made clear that my studies come first in this regard.
